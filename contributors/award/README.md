# Karmada Community Annual Contribution Awards

The Karmada Community Annual Contribution Awards are an annual recognition program established by the Karmada community to acknowledge and thank individuals and organizations that have made outstanding contributions to the community and the project over the past year. The awards recognize two categories: **Annual Contributors** (individual contributors) and **Annual Contributing Organizations** (companies/organizations that support the community). The selection is conducted by a community **Selection Committee** based on contribution statistics and nominations.

## Selection Committee

The Selection Committee is responsible for nominations and voting, ensuring that the process is fair, impartial, and transparent.

### Committee composition

The Selection Committee consists of:

- Karmada Community [Maintainers](https://github.com/karmada-io/community/blob/main/MAINTAINERS.md)

Committee members should remain objective and impartial, and evaluate nominees based on their actual contributions.

## Awards

The Karmada Community Annual Contribution Awards include two awards, recognizing individuals and organizations that have made notable contributions to community building and project development.

### 1. Annual Contributor

Thank you to individual contributors who actively participate and contribute consistently in the community.

- **Quota**: 10 recipients (adjustable based on actual needs)
- **Who**: Individuals who have made important contributions to the Karmada community, including but not limited to code contributions, documentation improvements, community operations, and technical evangelism.

**Recognition and gifts**:
1. Official Karmada community announcement
2. Annual Contributor certificate (electronic)
3. Community swag (the specific form may vary by year)

### 2. Annual Contributing Organization

Thank you to organizations that continue to invest resources and actively support the growth of the Karmada project.

- **Quota**: 10 organizations (adjustable based on actual needs)
- **Who**: Organizations that provide sustained support to the Karmada community, including but not limited to engineering time, technical support, case study sharing, and ecosystem building.

**Recognition**:
1. Official Karmada community announcement
2. Annual Contributing Organization certificate (electronic)

## Eligibility

The Karmada community recognizes that every contributor and every organization supports the community in different ways, and all of them are valuable.

### Individual nominee eligibility

We evaluate outstanding contributions across the dimensions below (meeting any one dimension is sufficient for nomination):

- **Contribution quality**: quality of code implementation, documentation, and community proposals
  - Examples: sound design, feature completeness, documentation accuracy, proposal value, etc.

- **Contribution impact**: positive impact on the community and the cloud-native multi-cluster ecosystem
  - Examples: technical talks, academic papers, community reviews, production adoption, mentoring at community events, etc.

- **Collaboration**: collaborative spirit and ownership demonstrated during community contributions
  - Examples: helping others, responding quickly and resolving issues effectively, providing constructive input in technical discussions, delivering work efficiently, and actively sharing knowledge, etc.

> Maintainers are core stewards of the project and take key responsibilities for the project's success. To provide more active contributors with recognition and encouragement, the Annual Contribution Awards focus on outstanding contributors outside the Maintainers team.

### Organization nominee eligibility

We evaluate outstanding organizational contributions across the dimensions below (meeting any one dimension is sufficient for nomination):

- **Organization investment**: resources and engineering time provided to the community
  - Examples: engineering time investment, technical resources support, etc.

- **Organization impact**: positive impact on the community and the cloud-native multi-cluster ecosystem
  - Examples: case study sharing, technical innovation, contributions to ecosystem building, etc.

- **Organization collaboration**: collaboration and shared responsibility with the community
  - Examples: actively participating in community activities, responding to community needs in a timely manner, providing constructive feedback, etc.

## Selection Process

The selection is nomination-based. The process is open and transparent: the criteria, process, and results are shared with the community and open to community oversight.

Selection Committee members should remain objective and impartial, and evaluate nominees based on their actual contributions, without being influenced by any external factors.

### Timeline

- **Kickoff**: end of the year
- **Nomination period**: 2–4 weeks
- **Voting period**: 2–4 weeks
- **Announcement**: announced immediately after the voting period ends

### Nomination

Nominations are conducted publicly via issues in the [community](https://github.com/karmada-io/community/issues) repository. Separate issues will be created for the two awards, and community members are welcome to participate and provide oversight.

There is no limit on the number of nominees during the nomination period. We welcome and encourage nominations for outstanding contributors and organizations.

**Who can nominate**

- Community members (including Selection Committee members) may nominate any contributor/organization, or self-nominate.

**How to nominate**

Nominate an Annual Contributor or an organization in a comment on the issue. One nominee per comment, in the format:

```
Nominee GitHub ID (or organization name) + nomination reason
```

### Voting

Voting is conducted via an online form by the Selection Committee. Each committee member may cast up to the award quota. The online form is shared within the Selection Committee and is not public.

After the voting period ends, no further votes are accepted. Committee members who did not vote are considered to have abstained.

Exceptional cases:
- **Not enough nominees**: if the number of valid nominees is below the quota, the committee will handle it based on the actual situation.

### Announcement

Awards will be announced in alphabetical order. Our sincere thanks and congratulations to all award recipients!
